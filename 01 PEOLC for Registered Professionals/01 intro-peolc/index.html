Core Concepts

What is Palliative & End of Life Care?

Understanding what palliative care is — and crucially, what it is not — is the foundation for compassionate, well-timed clinical communication.

Palliative and End of Life Care focuses on improving quality of life for people with serious, life-limiting illness.
🧠
Holistic Needs

Addresses physical, psychological, social and spiritual needs — treating the whole person, not just the illness.

👨‍👩‍👧
Family & Carers

Supports families and carers — their wellbeing, their grief, and their ability to provide care are all part of the focus.

⚕️
Alongside Active Treatment

Can be provided alongside curative or life-prolonging treatment. It is not a replacement for active care — it complements it.

📅
Not Just the Final Days

Is not limited to the final days or weeks of life. Palliative care can begin at diagnosis and continues through bereavement.

Common misconceptions — what palliative care is not
✗ It is not about "giving up" on a patient
✗ It is not only for people who are imminently dying
✗ It does not mean stopping all active treatment
✗ It is not only delivered in a hospice setting

Regulatory & Clinical Guidance

GMC & NICE Definitions

Understanding the formal definitions underpins consistent, legally grounded clinical communication.

📅
The 12-Month Definition

Patients are 'approaching the end of life' when they are likely to die within the next 12 months. This includes patients whose death is imminent (expected within a few hours or days) and a range of broader clinical circumstances.

GMC & NICE Combined Guidance

Patients are 'approaching the end of life' when they are likely to die within the next 12 months. This includes patients whose death is imminent (expected within a few hours or days) and those with advanced, progressive, incurable conditions.

The following groups are included within the 12-month definition
A
Advanced & Progressive

Advanced, progressive, incurable conditions — where disease trajectory indicates the person is likely to die within 12 months.

B
General Frailty

General frailty and co-existing conditions that mean the person is expected to die within 12 months.

C
Risk of Acute Crisis

Existing conditions where the person is at risk of dying from a sudden acute crisis in their condition.

D
Catastrophic Events

Life-threatening acute conditions caused by sudden catastrophic events, such as trauma or stroke.
