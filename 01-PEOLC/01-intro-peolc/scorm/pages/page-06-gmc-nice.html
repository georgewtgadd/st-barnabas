GMC & NICE: The 12-Month Definition

Understanding the clinical guidance that defines when a patient is 'approaching end of life'.

📅
The 12-Month Marker

Both the GMC and NICE define a patient as 'approaching end of life' when they are likely to die within the next 12 months. This definition guides referral decisions and triggers access to palliative support services.

General Medical Council (GMC)
Patients are 'approaching the end of life' when they are likely to die within the next 12 months. This includes patients whose death is imminent (expected within a few hours or days) and those with advanced, progressive, incurable conditions.
Four criteria for identifying patients approaching end of life
A
Advanced & Progressive

Advanced, progressive, incurable conditions — e.g. advanced cancer, heart failure, COPD, dementia — where death within 12 months is likely.

B
General Frailty

General frailty with co-existing conditions meaning the person is expected to die within 12 months.

C
Risk of Acute Crisis

Existing conditions where the person is at risk of dying from a sudden acute crisis in their condition.

D
Catastrophic Events

Life-threatening acute conditions caused by a sudden catastrophic event — for example, a major trauma or stroke.

Why this matters: Using the term 'palliative care' too late — or avoiding it altogether — can limit a person's access to symptom control, advance care planning, and psychosocial support. The 12-month definition is a tool to help you act earlier and more confidently.
