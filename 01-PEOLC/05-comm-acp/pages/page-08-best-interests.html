The Concept of "Best Interests"

When a patient lacks capacity and there is no valid ADRT or registered LPA, clinicians must act in the patient's best interests under Section 4 of the MCA (2005).

What "Best Interests" means in practice
🕐
Not solely a clinical decision

Best Interests is not simply what the clinician thinks is medically optimal. It must also consider the person's past wishes, beliefs, values, and what they would most likely have wanted.

👥
Consultation is mandatory

The clinician must consult anyone named by the patient as someone to be consulted, as well as those caring for the patient or interested in their welfare — including family members.

📋
A process, not a form

Best Interests is a structured decision-making process — not merely a box to tick. It must be documented clearly, showing the reasoning, who was consulted, and what evidence was considered.

⚖️
Least restrictive option

Before acting, the decision-maker must consider whether there is a less restrictive alternative that would achieve the same outcome and better preserve the person's rights and freedoms.

Section 4 MCA Checklist — considerations for Best Interests
Consider all relevant circumstances, not just the immediate clinical picture.
Consider whether the person might regain capacity — and if so, whether the decision can wait.
Take account of the person's past and present wishes, feelings, beliefs and values.
Consider any written statements the person made when they had capacity (e.g. advance statements).
Consult anyone named by the person, carers, close family, and friends — and anyone with a registered LPA.
For serious decisions with no appropriate consultee, instruct an Independent Mental Capacity Advocate (IMCA).
Document the process — including who was consulted, what was considered, and what was decided.

IMCA — a critical safeguard: Where there is no one appropriate to consult (other than paid carers), and the decision involves serious medical treatment or a change of accommodation, instructing an Independent Mental Capacity Advocate is a legal requirement under Section 37–39 of the MCA. The IMCA represents the person's interests — they are not a decision-maker, but a safeguard.
