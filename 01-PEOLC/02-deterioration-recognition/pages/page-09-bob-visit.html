📍 Setting the Scene

You are visiting Bob for a planned follow-up visit. This is the second time you have seen him. Bob answers the door himself and walks slowly to his chair in the living room. He is sitting upright, with his dog settled on his lap. He is dressed appropriately, makes eye contact, and chats politely. At first glance, he appears well.

While talking to Bob, you notice a yellowish tinge around his eyes, although you briefly wonder whether this could be due to the lighting in the room. You ask Bob how he has been.

Bob replies:
"I'm fine really. You know… plodding along."

He smiles slightly and changes the subject to his dog. Bob does not volunteer any specific concerns.

💡 Learning Focus
  • People often minimise symptoms.
  • "I'm fine" does not equal "nothing is wrong".
  • Deterioration is frequently uncovered through curiosity, not observation alone.

Key observations & questions to explore
  • Jaundice: The yellowish tinge suggests liver dysfunction or biliary obstruction — worth exploring directly.
  • Reduced mobility: He walked slowly — ask about energy levels and what a typical day looks like.
  • Subjective minimisation: "Plodding along" is vague. Probe gently: "What does plodding along actually look like day to day?"
  • Total Pain: Ask about appetite, sleep, pain, breathlessness, and emotional wellbeing — not just physical symptoms.
