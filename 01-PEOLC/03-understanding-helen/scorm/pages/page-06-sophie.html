Family Context

Sophie's Story

Sophie is 14 years old. Three years ago, she watched her mother Claire die of ovarian cancer. She was 11. She never really spoke about it — and nobody pushed her to.

Now her grandmother Helen is facing the same illness. The same breathlessness. The same oxygen tank in the corner. Sophie recognises all of it — and it terrifies her.

Her bedroom door stays shut. She has stopped eating with the family. Her teachers have noticed she is withdrawing at school. Simon is too exhausted to know how to reach her, and Helen is too worried about being a burden to ask.

"

She won't talk to me. I don't know if she's grieving Claire all over again, or if she's already grieving me. Maybe both.

— Helen, speaking to her community nurse
Consider as you plan Helen's care
  • Sophie is experiencing anticipatory grief — grief for a loss that hasn't happened yet, layered on top of unresolved grief for Claire.
  • Under the Family & Carer pillar, Sophie is a child in the home who requires specific support — a referral to youth bereavement services should be recorded in the care plan.
  • Helen's care preferences must account for Sophie's presence — where she dies, and how, will form part of Sophie's memory of her.
  • The Psychological & Spiritual pillar applies to the whole family — not just Helen.
