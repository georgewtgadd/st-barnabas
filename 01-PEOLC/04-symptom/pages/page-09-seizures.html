Managing Seizures

Seizures in palliative patients may occur due to brain metastases, metabolic disturbance, or medication. Management requires a calm, structured approach — particularly in community and hospice settings.

⏱️
When to Intervene
✓A seizure lasting <5 minutes — observe, keep safe, no emergency medication needed
!A seizure lasting ≥5 minutes constitutes status epilepticus — act immediately
!Two seizures without recovery between them also requires immediate intervention
!Always review anticipatory prescribing and inform the palliative care team after any seizure

    Record the duration and nature of the seizure. Inform the palliative care team. Review anticipatory prescribing to prevent recurrence.

    Administer Buccal Midazolam (or SC if access is ready)
    First-line anticonvulsant in community and hospice settings — typically 10mg buccal. Follow the prescribed anticipatory protocol.
  • ⠿
    Safe Environment & Airway
    Move hazards away, do not restrain. Place in recovery position after the tonic-clonic phase. Check airway patency.
  • ⠿
    Check Blood Glucose (BM)
    Exclude hypoglycaemia as a reversible, treatable cause before administering any anticonvulsant medication.
