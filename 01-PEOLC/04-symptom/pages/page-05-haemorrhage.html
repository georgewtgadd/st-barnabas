Terminal Haemorrhage

Catastrophic haemorrhage is rare but can occur when a tumour erodes a major vessel — most commonly in head and neck cancers. It requires a calm, structured response focused on patient comfort and dignity.

🩸
At-Risk Patients
!Head and neck cancer — particularly carotid artery involvement
!History of haemoptysis (coughing blood) or melaena
!Anticipatory medication (Midazolam IM/SC) must already be prescribed
!Dark-coloured towels should be kept available in the home
