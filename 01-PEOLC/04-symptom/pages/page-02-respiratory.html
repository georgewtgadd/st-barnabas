Managing the Respiratory Tract

Breathlessness (dyspnoea) and terminal secretions are two of the most distressing respiratory symptoms in palliative care — often more frightening for the family than for the patient themselves.

💧
Terminal Secretions — the "Death Rattle"

As patients become unable to swallow, secretions pool in the throat producing a gurgling sound. Research consistently shows this is rarely distressing to the unconscious patient — but it can be deeply upsetting for families. Your role includes explaining this physiology clearly and sensitively.
