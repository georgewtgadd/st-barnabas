Clinical & Ethical Dilemmas

Managing diabetes at end of life is rarely straightforward. Before continuing, take a moment to reflect on the key tensions in this area of practice.

⚖️
The Dilemma

Balancing the avoidance of acute complications — hypoglycaemia or DKA — against the burden of invasive monitoring in a dying patient.

🔄
Key Conflict

Shifting from long-term preventative care to immediate symptom control and comfort — the entire clinical goal changes.

🧭
Decision Making

Planning is often seen as a choice between treating or withdrawing treatment, but in practice, caring for the dying individual is more complex — it is about modulation, not abandonment.

Your Reflection

In your experience, what is the most challenging aspect of diabetes management at end of life? Consider both the clinical and the human dimensions.
