🏥 EoL Adjustments to These Targets
Relaxed Targets

In EoL care, the NICE targets above should be relaxed. Many clinicians accept glucose levels of 6–15 mmol/L in the last weeks of life, provided the patient is symptom-free. Tight control is no longer the goal.

What to Avoid

Avoid glucose below 4 mmol/L (hypoglycaemia — causes distressing symptoms) and sustained levels above 20 mmol/L (risk of DKA in Type 1, symptomatic hyperglycaemia in Type 2).

Monitoring Frequency

In the last days of life, routine monitoring may be reduced to once daily or discontinued if the patient is no longer taking insulin and is not symptomatic. Testing should always be clinically indicated.

HbA1c is Irrelevant

HbA1c reflects 3-month average glucose and guides long-term management. At end of life, this target becomes clinically meaningless — it should not be used to guide EoL dosing decisions.
