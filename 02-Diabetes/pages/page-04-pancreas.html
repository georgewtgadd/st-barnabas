Anatomy

The Pancreas

A unique organ serving two entirely separate systems — understanding this dual role is essential to understanding how different types of diabetes arise.

🩸
Endocrine Gland
Insulin & Glucagon

Secretes hormones directly into the bloodstream via the Islets of Langerhans. These hormones — primarily insulin and glucagon — regulate blood glucose across the entire body. This is the system affected by diabetes.

EoL Relevance Insulin-dependent patients cannot survive without some exogenous insulin — complete withdrawal causes DKA within hours to days.
🍽
Exocrine Gland
Digestive Enzymes

Produces digestive enzymes (amylase, lipase, proteases) that flow through ducts directly into the duodenum. These break down carbohydrates, fats, and proteins in food. Damage to this system (e.g. pancreatitis) can also disrupt glucose control.

EoL Relevance Pancreatic cancer affecting the exocrine tissue can simultaneously destroy Islets — causing Type 3c diabetes.
