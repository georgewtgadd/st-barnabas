EoL Clinical
Management

When a patient reaches the end of life, the entire goal of diabetes management changes. This module focuses on safe, compassionate rationalisation of therapy — protecting patients from preventable harm while keeping comfort at the centre of every decision.

📋

Work through each section in order. The module includes a blood glucose zones reference, two drag-and-drop activities, sequential hypo scenarios, an insulin dose simulator, and a five-question MCQ — with a full Learning Record at the end.

⏱ ~25 mins  ·  10 pages  ·  5 interactive activities
Learning Outcomes
By the end of this module you will be able to
1
Apply

Appropriate glycaemic targets for patients in the dying phase of illness

2
Rationalise

Non-insulin drug therapies to minimise risk and reduce unnecessary pill burden at end of life

3
Identify & Manage

Hypoglycaemia and hyperglycaemic emergencies (DKA/HHS) in a palliative setting

4
Adjust

Insulin doses for Type 1 patients as consciousness and oral intake decline

5
Explain

Why patient-centred values and Advance Care Plans must guide all clinical decisions at end of life
