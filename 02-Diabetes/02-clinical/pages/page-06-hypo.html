Emergency Management

Managing Hypoglycaemia

Hypoglycaemia is the most common and preventable metabolic emergency at end of life. Recognition and rapid response are essential — it is distressing, reversible, and can be avoided entirely with appropriate medication review.

🚨
Recognise
Signs & Symptoms
  • Anxiety and agitation
  • Sweating — often cold, clammy skin
  • Hunger (early sign)
  • Confusion and disorientation
  • Tremors and shakiness
  • Pallor
  • Seizures (severe — below 2.0 mmol/L)
  • Loss of consciousness (severe)
💊
Manage
Step-by-Step Response
1

Test blood glucose if equipment is available and testing is in the care plan. Confirm before treating.

2

If the patient can swallow safely: give quick-acting glucose — Lucozade, fruit juice, glucose tablets, or sugary drink (150–200ml).

3

If the patient cannot swallow and it aligns with the care plan: consider Glucagon IM/SC or call 999 for 10% IV Glucose via ambulance.

4

Review medications: a hypoglycaemic episode is a clear signal to reduce or stop insulin/sulphonylurea doses. Document and notify the prescriber.

Risk Factors — Hypoglycaemia at End of Life
Poor appetite or reduced oral intake
Progressive weight loss
Renal deterioration (reduced drug clearance)
Use of sulphonylureas
Insulin — especially long-acting preparations
Erratic or absent eating pattern
