Medication Review & Safety

A structured medication review is essential when a patient enters the terminal phase. Many drugs that were once vital now carry more risk than benefit.

Medication Class Primary Risk at End of Life Recommended Action
Sulphonylureas
(e.g. Gliclazide)
High Hypo Risk
Stimulates insulin secretion regardless of food intake. High risk of severe hypoglycaemia when eating poorly.		 Stop when oral intake falls or in the terminal phase.
SGLT2 Inhibitors
(e.g. Dapagliflozin)
DKA Risk
Can cause euglycaemic DKA (ketoacidosis with near-normal glucose). Risk increases with dehydration and poor intake.		 Stop when oral intake falls or in the terminal phase.
Metformin
Renal Risk
Excreted by the kidneys. Risk of lactic acidosis if renal function declines significantly (common at end of life).		 Stop if renal function falls or in the terminal phase.
Statins / Antihypertensives
No Benefit
Preventative drugs with no immediate benefit in the terminal phase. Adds to pill burden and potential side effects.		 Stop in the terminal phase to reduce pill burden.
Basal Insulin
(e.g. Lantus, Levemir)
Physiological Need
In Type 1, insulin is essential to prevent DKA. Stopping it causes rapid, distressing deterioration.		 Continue (dose-reduced) in Type 1 until unconscious.
⚠️
The Golden Rule of Type 1
Never stop insulin in a Type 1 patient without specialist advice. Even if they are not eating, they still require basal insulin to prevent DKA. Doses should be reduced — but not withdrawn.
