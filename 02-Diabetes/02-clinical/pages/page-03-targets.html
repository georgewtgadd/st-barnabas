Glycaemic Targets at End of Life

For patients in the terminal phase, glucose targets are deliberately relaxed to ensure comfort. The priority is avoiding distressing symptoms at both extremes — not achieving a perfect reading.

🩸
Testing frequency

Keep testing to an absolute minimum. Finger-prick testing is distressing. In the final days, once daily — or only when symptomatic — is usually sufficient.

📋
Symptom-led monitoring

If the patient appears unusually confused, shaky, or distressed, test blood glucose even outside the regular schedule. Symptoms trump the timetable.

❌
What to avoid

Avoid glucose below 4.0 mmol/L (symptomatic hypoglycaemia) and sustained levels above 20 mmol/L (risk of DKA in Type 1, severe hyperglycaemic symptoms in Type 2).
