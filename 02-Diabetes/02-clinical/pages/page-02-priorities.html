Core Principles

Shifting Priorities in Care

As a patient enters the end-of-life phase, the entire goal of diabetes management changes. Understanding this shift is the foundation of everything that follows.

⚖️
From long-term prevention → to immediate comfort and dignity

The clinical evidence base for tight glycaemic control is built on prevention of complications over years and decades. At end of life, those timescales no longer apply. The entire evidence base changes.

📉
Reduced Priority
Relaxed Glucose Targets

Tight "normal" glucose ranges are no longer the goal. Long-term complications are irrelevant when the prognosis is weeks to months. Aggressive monitoring causes unnecessary distress.

🩺
Primary Focus
Symptom Control

The goal is to avoid uncomfortable symptoms of hyperglycaemia — thirst, frequent urination, fatigue, and blurred vision — not to achieve a number on a glucometer.

⚠️
Critical Priority
Hypoglycaemia Avoidance

Hypoglycaemia is distressing, potentially dangerous, and entirely preventable with appropriate medication review. It is the single most important metabolic risk to manage proactively.

🍽
Wellbeing
Dietary Freedom

Strict dietary restrictions are unlikely to be appropriate. Patients should be encouraged to eat what they enjoy, when they can. Food is comfort — not a clinical variable to control.
